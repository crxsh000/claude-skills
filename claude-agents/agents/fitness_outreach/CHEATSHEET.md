# CHEATSHEET v3 — Dual-Mode DM Conversion System

---

## OPERATING MODES — READ FIRST

This system has TWO modes. Every script, flow, and deliverable must be tagged.

| Mode | What It Is | Who Initiates | Outbound Allowed? |
|------|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **MODE A** | Your outbound acquisition (to sign coaches as clients) | You | Yes — respectful, personalized, non-spammy |
| **MODE B** | Client inbound conversion (warm leads → booked calls) | Lead | No — inbound/opt-in only |

**Before generating any script, ask: "Is this MODE A or MODE B?"**

## 1. WHAT WE DO (Positioning + Scope)

### The Core Offer (MODE B — For Clients)

> "We convert your warm inbound DMs into booked qualified calls. You close."

### What We DO

| Service | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| DM Triage | Separate warm inbound from noise |
| Qualification | ICP fit + pain/goal + urgency + decision-maker + budget plausibility |
| Booking | Schedule calls onto coach's calendar |
| Follow-Ups | Reschedules, reminders, nudges |
| Show-Up Support | Reminders + no-show recovery |
| Reporting | Weekly: reply → qualify → booked → show |

### What We DON'T Do

| Exclusion | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Close sales calls | Coach responsibility — we book, they close |
| Run ads | Not our service |
| Cold outreach for clients | Inbound only — warm leads only |
| Create content | No writing posts, filming, editing, posting |

### Optional Add-On (Content Planning)

We CAN provide:
- Content angles + hooks
- CTA optimization ideas
- Weekly content calendar
- Topic ideation to increase inbound volume

We do NOT create the content itself.

## 2. DEFINITIONS BOX

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Warm Inbound** | User-initiated interaction: inbound DM, story reply, comment/keyword trigger, quick reply/button tap |
| **Booked Call** | Calendar slot confirmed with reminder sent |
| **Qualified Call** | Passes all 6 criteria (see below) |
| **No-Show** | Didn't attend within 10 min of scheduled time |
| **Client Responsibilities** | Close calls, respond to approval requests within 24h, provide Closer Kit feedback |

### Qualified Call Checklist (All Must Be True)

1. ✅ ICP fit confirmed
2. ✅ Pain/goal confirmed
3. ✅ Active intent confirmed ("looking for help now")
4. ✅ Decision-maker confirmed
5. ✅ Budget plausibility confirmed (range/ability)
6. ✅ Call booked within 7 days

If any box is unchecked, it's not a qualified call.

## 3. PILOT GUARANTEE + SUCCESS CONDITIONS

### The Guarantee (MODE B)

> "14-Day Pilot: We will book 10 QUALIFIED calls within 14 days from your WARM inbound.
> If we don't hit 10 by Day 14, we continue working at no cost until 10 QUALIFIED calls are booked — as long as Success Conditions are met."

### Success Conditions (Must Be Met)

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Inbound Volume** | Minimum [X] warm inbound convos in past 14 days (ask for screenshot/data) |
| **Calendar Capacity** | At least 10 call slots available within 14 days |
| **Closer Kit Used** | Coach uses provided scripts and handles sales calls |
| **Response SLA** | Client approves/clarifies requests within 24 hours |
| **Compliance Acknowledged** | Client understands messaging window constraints |

**If conditions aren't met, guarantee doesn't apply.**

### What Happens If Inbound Is Too Low?

Honest conversation:
> "Based on your current inbound volume ([X] convos/week), we can't promise 10 qualified calls in 14 days. Options: (1) we start with what you have and track performance, (2) we add content planning to increase inbound first, or (3) we wait until volume is higher."

#### Implication (I) — Pick 1-2
| # | Question |
|---|----------|
| I1 | "How is that affecting your revenue month to month?" |
| I2 | "How much time are you spending on DMs vs actually coaching?" |

| # | Question |
|---|----------|

| N2 | "What would it mean to know every call on your calendar was someone ready to buy?" |
| N3 | "If DM management wasn't your problem, what would you focus on instead?" |

### Reflect-Back Lines (Use After 2 Questions)
- "So if I'm hearing you right — [summary]. That accurate?"
- "Sounds like the main thing is [X]. Am I getting that right?"
- "So [situation] and that's causing [implication]. Correct?"

### Branching Flows

#### Flow A: Replies But No Bookings
**Signals:** Getting DMs, but calls aren't happening
**Priority Questions:** S4, P2, P3, I2, N1
**Angle:** "Your content is doing the hard part — there's no conversion system on the back end."

#### Flow B: Bookings But No-Shows
**Signals:** Calls booked, people don't show
**Priority Questions:** S3, P3, I1, N2
**Angle:** "The people booking aren't qualified. When someone confirms intent first, show rates jump."

#### Flow C: Low Inbound Volume / DMs Quiet
**Signals:** Not enough conversations starting
**Priority Questions:** S1, S4, P1, P4
**Honest Next Steps:**
> "If inbound is low, we can help with content planning to increase it — but we'd be straight with you: without volume, we can't promise results. Want to start there first?"

> "Quick chat? 15 min. I'll ask about your setup, you ask about mine. If it's not a fit, we'll both know. Fair?"

>
> At the end, one of three things: it makes sense and we move forward, it doesn't and we part as friends, or you need more info and we figure that out.
>
> Sound fair?"

> "No pressure call — just 15 min to see if this makes sense for you. If it doesn't, I'll tell you. You should tell me too. Either way, we'll know. Cool?"

- "If it's not a fit, that's fine — we'll both know."
- "This isn't a convince-you call. It's a figure-out-fit call."
- "If you get on and it's not for you, just say so. No hard feelings."

## 13. FOLLOW-UP SYSTEM

### Structure: Each FU adds 1 new insight + 1 proof element + ends with A/B question

### FU1 (48 hours)

**Template:**
> "One thing I noticed — [new observation]. [One insight]. [Proof line]. Does that match what you're seeing, or is your situation different?"

**Example:**
> "One thing I noticed — your engagement dropped off on posts without a CTA. Coaches who add a simple 'DM me X' see 2-3x more convos start. A nutrition coach I worked with tested this and went from 4 to 12 DMs/week. Is CTA something you've experimented with, or not really?"

### FU2 (5 days after FU1)

**Template:**
> "Thought about your situation. [Case study]. [Insight]. Still exploring this, or did you go a different direction?"

**Example:**
> "Thought about your situation. Worked with a coach in [similar niche] who had the same issue — great content, no calls. After we added qualification, show rate went from 55% to 82%. The fix wasn't more leads — it was better filtering. Still thinking about this, or did you figure out another path?"

### FU3 (10 days after FU2) — Clean Exit

**Template:**
> "I'll let you be — you know your situation best. If the [relevant problem] becomes a priority, I'm around. Good luck with [something specific about their work]."

**Example:**
> "I'll let you be — you know your situation best. If the no-show thing becomes a priority, I'm around. Good luck with the busy-mom niche — it's a solid lane."

### Exit Rules
- After FU3, stop. No exceptions.
- No guilt language ("guess you're not interested")
- If they reply at any point, restart fresh

## 14. COMPLIANCE & DM ETIQUETTE

### Opt-In Requirements (MODE B)

Warm inbound = user-initiated. Valid triggers:
- Inbound DM (they messaged first)
- Story reply
- Comment/keyword that triggers DM opt-in
- Quick reply / button tap
- Form submission

**Not valid:** Following, liking, viewing story (without reply)

### Messaging Window Constraints

| Scenario | Rule |
|----------|------|
| Within 24 hours of their last message | Automations allowed |
| After 24 hours of no reply | Automations stop; manual message may be possible depending on platform |
| After 7 days of no reply | Most platforms block; FU3 and exit |

**Reality:** Meta enforces these windows. Don't promise unlimited automation.

### Pacing Rules

| Metric | Limit |
|--------|-------|
| New DMs per account per day (MODE A) | 20-30 max |
| Time between DMs | 2-5 minutes minimum |
| Follow-ups per prospect | 3 max |
| Time between follow-ups | 48+ hours |

### When to Stop Immediately
- They say "stop," "not interested," "don't contact me"
- They block or report
- They've said no twice in any form
- They're clearly annoyed ("k," "ok," ignoring questions)

### Graceful Exit Scripts
> "No problem — appreciate you hearing me out. Good luck with everything."

> "Totally fair. If anything changes, feel free to reach out. Take care."

> "Got it — timing's off. I'll leave you be. Best with [their niche]."

### What Never to Do
- Mass DM identical messages
- Use automation that sends while "offline"
- Follow-unfollow tactics
- Fake engagement before DMing
- Lie about mutual connections
- Promise "guaranteed revenue"

### Module 1: Intake Questions

1. "Is this MODE A (your outbound) or MODE B (client inbound)?"

### Module 2: Personalization Engine

Extract before every DM:
- Niche + target client
- Offer type + price signals
- Content style
- Current CTA
- Biggest visible leak
- Latest non-pinned post detail

### Module 3: Diagnosis-First Flow

Every conversation must follow:
```
OBSERVATION → PERMISSION → 1-LINE DIAGNOSIS → TINY WIN → QUESTION
```

No pitching until 2+ discovery questions answered.

Every pitch message must include exactly ONE proof line:
- Metric ("show rate went from 55% to 82%")
- Mini case ("worked with a nutrition coach who...")
- Named mechanism ("we use a 3-question qualification filter")

Hard No criteria:
- No proven offer yet
- Can't take calls this month
- Sells only $50-200 products
- Ghosted last 2 follow-ups
- Wants "guaranteed revenue" with no risk
- Inbound volume too low for guarantee (and unwilling to address)

Exit script:
> "Sounds like timing might be off. Happy to reconnect in [X weeks] if things shift."

For every qualified prospect, generate:
- 3 hook variations (mix of Tier A and B if appropriate)

- 1 voice note script
- 1 price response
- 1 call transition

### Module 7: Continuous Improvement Loop

Weekly process:
1. Review all hooks — rank by reply %

5. Document why winners won / losers lost
