# Conversation Flows by Offer Type

Complete conversation flows from hook to call booking. Reference these when DMing prospects.

---

## How to Use This Document

1. Identify the prospect's offer type (No Offer, 1:1, Program, App)
2. Find the relevant section below
3. Use the hook template, personalize it
4. Follow the flow based on their responses
5. Adjust based on what they actually say (these are guides, not scripts)

**Key Rules:**
- Ask 2+ discovery questions before ANY pitch
- Let them talk more than you
- If they say no at any point, exit clean
- Never send 3+ messages without a reply

---

## OFFER TYPE 1: NO CLEAR OFFER

**Profile signs:** Bio says "coach" or "trainer" but no link, no program mentioned, no CTA, just content.

**Your goal:** Qualify if they even sell coaching, then pivot to discovery if yes.

---

### Flow N1: The Qualification Check

**HOOK:**
> "Your [specific post topic] content is solid. Quick q — are you coaching people, or just building an audience right now?"

**THEM (Option A - Yes they coach):**
> "Yeah I do 1:1 coaching!"

**YOU (Discovery Q1):**
> "Nice. How are most of your clients finding you right now — through your content or somewhere else?"

**THEM:**
> "Mostly through IG, some referrals"

**YOU (Discovery Q2):**
> "Got it. When someone DMs you interested, what's your process to figure out if they're serious?"

**THEM:**
> "I just talk to them and see if it's a fit"

**YOU (Diagnosis):**
> "Makes sense. A lot of coaches I talk to say the same — they end up spending a lot of time on people who aren't actually ready to buy. Is that something you run into?"

**THEM:**
> "Yeah actually, a lot of tire kickers"

**YOU (Tiny Win):**
> "Yeah that's super common. Usually it's because there's no filter before they get to you. The serious ones and the curious ones all look the same in the DMs."

**THEM:**
> "Exactly, how do you filter them?"

**YOU (Offer):**
> "I basically handle DMs for coaches — qualify people before they get to you so you're only talking to the ones who are actually ready. First 10 calls are free to prove it works. Would that be useful, or are you good handling it yourself?"

**THEM (Option B - No, just content):**
> "Just building audience for now, not coaching yet"

**YOU (Clean Exit):**
> "Makes sense — smart to build the audience first. Good luck with it!"

**STOP. Not a fit.**

### Flow N2: The Monetization Question

**HOOK:**
> "Saw your [specific post] — good stuff. Are you taking on clients, or focused on content for now?"

**THEM:**
> "I take clients but honestly not getting many inquiries"

**YOU (Discovery Q1):**
> "Got it. How are you getting the ones you do have right now?"

**THEM:**
> "Just people who see my content and DM me"

**YOU (Discovery Q2):**
> "Makes sense. How many DMs do you get in a typical week from people interested?"

**THEM:**
> "Like 2-3 maybe"

**YOU (Diagnosis):**
> "So the content is bringing some people in, but volume is the issue. Are you posting consistently, or is that part of the challenge too?"

**THEM:**
> "I try to post but it's hard to keep up"

**YOU (Honest Assessment):**
> "Yeah that's tough. Honestly, if inbound is only 2-3 a week, the first thing to solve is probably content consistency before anything else. Have you thought about batching content or having someone help with planning?"

**THEM:**
> "Not really, what do you mean?"

**YOU (Value, No Pitch):**
> "Like spending one day a week planning/recording a bunch, so you're not scrambling daily. There's some good frameworks out there. If you want I can point you to some resources — not selling anything, just might help with the volume issue first."

**If they want resources, share genuinely. If inbound is too low, they're not a fit for your DM service yet — but you can build relationship for later.**

## OFFER TYPE 2: 1:1 COACHING

**Profile signs:** Clearly offers 1:1 coaching, mentions "1:1" or "personal coaching" in bio, possibly shows price or "apply" CTA.

**Your angle:** Time is their bottleneck. They're coaching AND selling AND doing admin. Backend is eating their coaching hours.

---

**THEM:**
> "Exactly, I don't know who's actually serious until I've talked to them"

**YOU (Teach):**
> "Most coaches I work with had the same problem. The fix is usually asking qualifying questions early — are they ready to start now, do they have budget, are they the decision maker. The ones who answer those are usually 3-4x more likely to book and show."

**THEM:**

First 10 calls are free to prove it works. If they don't show or aren't qualified, I replace them. Would that be useful?"

You just show up to calls with people who are already sold on coaching, just deciding if you're the right fit. Want to hop on a quick call and I can show you exactly what it looks like?"

## OFFER TYPE 4: APP-BASED COACHING

**Your angle:** Engagement and churn. They get downloads but people don't stick. Or they get DMs asking "is this right for me?" and have no system to convert them.

---
