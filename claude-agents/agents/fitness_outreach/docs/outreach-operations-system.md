## 1.2 Prospecting Process: How to Find & Qualify Ideal Prospects

### STEP 1: Build Your Prospect Universe (Saturday + 15 min/day)

**Primary Sourcing Methods:**

1. HASHTAG MINING (Highest volume)
   - Search these hashtags and review recent posts:
     - #onlinefitnescoach / #onlinefitnescoaching
     - #fitnessbusinesscoach
     - #onlinepersonaltrainer / #onlinepersonaltraining
     - #fitnesscoachlife
     - #transformationcoach
     - #macrocoach / #nutritioncoach
     - #fitnessentrepreneur
     - #coachlife
   - Sort by RECENT, not top (top posts are usually accounts too large to fit the avatar)
   - Aim to pull 50-75 prospect profiles per sourcing session

2. COMPETITOR AUDIENCE MINING (Highest quality)
   - Identify 10-15 fitness business coaching accounts (these are people who SELL to your avatar)
   - Examples of account types to monitor:
     - Fitness business coaches
     - Online coaching course sellers
     - Fitness-specific CRM/software accounts
   - Mine their followers, commenters, and people who engage with their content
   - These prospects are pre-qualified: they already invest in growing their business

3. ENGAGEMENT-BASED DISCOVERY (Warmest leads)
   - Monitor who engages with YOUR client's content
   - Check story viewers
   - Review comments on posts about business growth, systems, or client results
   - These people have already shown interest -- they go to the TOP of the outreach list

4. EXPLORE PAGE & REELS DISCOVERY
   - Instagram's algorithm will surface fitness coach content if the account regularly engages with it
   - Scroll the explore page for 10 minutes daily and add qualifying profiles to the list

### STEP 2: Qualification Criteria (The 6-Point Filter)

Before sending a DM, every prospect MUST pass at least 5 of 6 criteria:

| # | Criteria | How to Verify | Pass/Fail |
|---|----------|---------------|-----------|
| 1 | Is an online fitness/nutrition coach | Bio says "coach," "trainer," or "helping [people] with [fitness goal]" | Required |
| 2 | Has 1,000 - 50,000 followers | Check follower count. Under 1k = too early. Over 50k = likely has a team already | Required |
| 3 | Posts consistently (2+ times/week) | Scroll their grid -- are they active in the last 7 days? | Required |
| 4 | Shows signs of being a solopreneur | No "team" page, no employee tags, responds to their own comments | Preferred |
| 5 | Located in US/Canada/UK/Australia | Check bio for location, link in bio for timezone clues | Preferred |
| 6 | Already sells coaching (has offers) | Bio link goes to coaching page, mentions "DM me," has testimonials | Preferred |

**Disqualification signals (immediate skip):**
- Bio says "agency" or "marketing"
- Follower count over 100k (different buyer psychology)
- No posts in 30+ days (inactive/abandoned account)
- Clearly a gym/brick-and-mortar business (not online)
- Already has a large team visible in content

### STEP 3: Pre-Outreach Warm-Up (The 48-Hour Rule)

Before sending any DM, engage with the prospect's content for 24-48 hours:

Day 1:
- Like 2-3 of their recent posts
- Leave 1 genuine comment on a post (NOT "great post" -- reference specific content)
- Watch their story and react to one slide

Day 2:
- Like 1-2 more posts
- Reply to a story with a genuine observation or question
- THEN send the opening DM

This warm-up step increases reply rates by an estimated 40-60% compared to
completely cold outreach. It also reduces the chance of being flagged as spam
because your DM arrives in the context of prior engagement.

## 1.4 Time Blocking Recommendations

### DAILY OUTREACH SCHEDULE (Total: 90-120 minutes/day)

**Block 1: Morning Prospecting (20-30 minutes) -- 8:00-8:30 AM**
- Review overnight replies and respond (10 min)
- Qualify and add new prospects to the list (10 min)
- Engage (like/comment) on tomorrow's DM targets (10 min)

**Block 2: Midday Outreach (45-60 minutes) -- 11:00 AM-12:00 PM**
- Send 15-20 new DMs (this is when fitness coaches check Instagram between client sessions)
- Send 8-12 follow-up messages
- Advance active conversations (reply to ongoing threads)

**Block 3: Afternoon Follow-Up (20-30 minutes) -- 4:00-5:00 PM**
- Send remaining 5-10 new DMs
- Send remaining follow-ups
- Respond to any afternoon replies
- Update tracking spreadsheet

**Block 4: Evening Engagement (10-15 minutes) -- 8:00-9:00 PM**
- Reply to any evening messages (fitness coaches are often active after their last client)
- Engage with prospect stories (this is prime story-posting time for fitness coaches)
- Do NOT send new cold DMs in the evening -- this feels intrusive

### WHY THESE TIMES MATTER
Online fitness coaches typically:
- Train their own body: 5:00-7:00 AM
- Check social media: 8:00-9:00 AM and 11:00 AM-1:00 PM
- Coach clients: 9:00-11:00 AM and 2:00-5:00 PM
- Create content: 5:00-7:00 PM
- Personal time + phone scrolling: 8:00-10:00 PM

Sending DMs during their natural "check social media" windows (late morning) yields
the fastest reply times, which keeps conversation momentum high.

## 1.5 Pipeline Stages

### THE 7-STAGE OUTREACH PIPELINE

```
STAGE 0: PROSPECT (Not yet contacted)
  |
  v
STAGE 1: WARM-UP (Engaging with their content, 24-48 hours)
  |
  v
STAGE 2: FIRST DM SENT (Opening message delivered)
  |
  v
STAGE 3: REPLIED (They responded -- conversation is active)
  |
  v
STAGE 4: QUALIFIED (Confirmed they fit the avatar + expressed interest/pain)
  |
  v
STAGE 5: CALL BOOKED (Discovery/strategy call scheduled)
  |
  v
STAGE 6: CALL COMPLETED (Had the conversation)
  |
  v
STAGE 7: CLOSED (Signed up) or LOST (Did not buy -- tag with reason)
```

### STAGE MOVEMENT RULES

**Stage 0 to Stage 1:**
- Trigger: Prospect passes 5/6 qualification criteria
- Action: Begin engaging with their content

**Stage 1 to Stage 2:**
- Trigger: 24-48 hours of engagement completed
- Action: Send personalized opening DM

**Stage 2 to Stage 3:**
- Trigger: They reply to the DM
- Action: Begin conversation framework (see Section 2)
- If no reply after 48 hours: Send Follow-Up #1
- If no reply after 5 days: Send Follow-Up #2
- If no reply after 10 days: Send Follow-Up #3 (final)
- If no reply after Follow-Up #3: Move to LOST (reason: no response)

**Stage 3 to Stage 4:**
- Trigger: Through conversation, confirmed they have the pain points AND financial capacity
- Action: Transition to call invitation
- Qualification must confirm: (1) they coach online, (2) they want to grow, (3) they can invest

**Stage 4 to Stage 5:**
- Trigger: They agree to a call and pick a time
- Action: Send calendar link, confirm booking, send pre-call prep message

**Stage 5 to Stage 6:**
- Trigger: Call happens
- Action: Run the sales call framework
- If they no-show: Send reschedule message within 5 minutes. Offer 2 alternative times.
  Second no-show: One final attempt. Third no-show: Move to LOST.

**Stage 6 to Stage 7:**
- Trigger: They say yes (CLOSED) or no (LOST)
- Action: If closed, begin onboarding. If lost, tag with objection reason for future analysis.
- If "need to think about it": Schedule 48-hour follow-up. Then 5-day. Then 10-day. Then LOST.

# SECTION 2: CONVERSION FRAMEWORK

## 2.1 Conversation Flow: Reply to Booked Call

### THE 5-MESSAGE FRAMEWORK

After someone replies to your initial DM, follow this conversation architecture.
The goal is NOT to sell in the DMs. The goal is to get them on a call.

**MESSAGE 1: ACKNOWLEDGE + CURIOSITY HOOK**
Purpose: Thank them for replying, ask an open-ended question that gets them talking about their business.

Example:
"Hey [Name], appreciate you getting back to me! I was actually looking at your page
and you clearly know your stuff when it comes to [specific thing from their content].
Quick question -- are you mostly working with clients 1-on-1 right now, or have you
started doing any group stuff?"

Key principles:
- Reference something SPECIFIC from their content (proves you are not copy-pasting)
- Ask about their business model (this reveals pain points naturally)
- Keep it conversational, not salesy

**MESSAGE 2: PAIN EXCAVATION**
Purpose: Once they answer, dig into their biggest challenge. Let THEM say the problem.

Example (if they say "mostly 1-on-1"):
"Nice, 1-on-1 is great for results but I imagine it gets pretty intense on your
time. What's the biggest bottleneck for you right now -- is it more on the getting
clients side or managing the ones you already have?"

Key principles:
- Reflect back what they said
- Offer a binary choice (this is easier to answer than open-ended at this stage)
- Both options are pain points your client solves -- either answer works

**MESSAGE 3: DEPTH + EMPATHY**
Purpose: Go deeper on whichever pain they identified. Show you understand their world.

Example (if they say "getting clients"):
"Yeah that's super common honestly. A lot of coaches I talk to are amazing at what
they do but the pipeline is either feast or famine -- some months are great, others
you're scrambling. Is that kind of what it feels like, or is it more that you just
don't have a system for it?"

Key principles:
- Use their exact words back to them
- Normalize the problem ("a lot of coaches I talk to...")
- Show you understand the EMOTIONAL experience, not just the tactical problem

**MESSAGE 4: BRIDGE TO VALUE**
Purpose: Transition from problem to possibility. Plant the seed that there is a solution.

Example:
"That makes total sense. So here's why I actually reached out -- we've been helping
coaches like you build a system that brings in booked calls every week without running
ads or burning their audience. Not gonna pitch you in the DMs obviously, but would
you be open to jumping on a quick 15-20 min call so I can show you how it works?
No pressure, worst case you walk away with some ideas you can use either way."

Key principles:
- "Here's why I actually reached out" -- creates a natural transition
- Mention the OUTCOME they want (booked calls, no ads, no audience burning)
- Explicitly say you are NOT pitching in the DMs (reduces resistance)
- Frame the call as low-risk: "quick 15-20 min," "no pressure," "walk away with ideas"

**MESSAGE 5: BOOKING LOGISTICS**
Purpose: If they say yes, immediately lock in a time. Speed matters here.

Example:
"Awesome! Here's my calendar link -- grab whatever time works best for you:
[link]. If none of those work just let me know a couple times and I'll make it happen."

Key principles:
- Send the link IMMEDIATELY (every hour of delay reduces booking probability)
- Offer flexibility ("if none work, let me know")
- After they book, send a confirmation: "Got you locked in for [day/time]!
  Quick heads up -- I'll send you a short 2-minute prep doc before the call so
  we can make the most of our time."

### CONVERSATION TIMING RULES
- Reply to their messages within 1-2 hours maximum during business hours
- If they reply at night, respond first thing the next morning
- Never send more than 2 messages in a row without a reply (this feels desperate)
- If they go silent mid-conversation: Wait 24 hours, then send a low-pressure nudge
- Match their energy and message length (if they send short replies, keep yours shorter)

## 2.2 Qualification Criteria

### THE "READY, WILLING, ABLE" FRAMEWORK

A prospect must be all three to be worth a call:

**READY (They have the problem NOW)**
- They are actively coaching clients online
- They express frustration with lead flow, income inconsistency, or admin overwhelm
- They are posting content and trying to grow (not dormant)
- Signals: "Yeah I've been struggling with that," "That's exactly my situation"

**WILLING (They are open to a solution)**
- They engaged with your DM (replied with more than one word)
- They asked questions about what you do
- They did not immediately say "I'm not interested" or "I don't have budget"
- Signals: "How does that work?" "What do you mean by system?" "Tell me more"

**ABLE (They can invest)**
- They are making at least $2k/month from coaching (if below this, they likely cannot afford your client's services)
- They are not locked into a competing service/agency
- They have decision-making authority (it is their business)
- Signals: They talk about having clients, mention revenue, discuss investing in their business

### RED FLAGS (Do NOT book a call)
- "I'm just starting out and don't have any clients yet" (not ready)
- "I don't really have a budget for anything right now" (not able)
- "I've been burned by 3 agencies and I'll never hire anyone again" (not willing -- at least not now)
- "Can you just tell me what you'd do over DM?" (wants free consulting, not a buyer)
- One-word replies consistently ("cool" "nice" "ok") (not engaged enough)

### GRACEFUL DISQUALIFICATION
If someone is not qualified, do NOT ghost them. Say something like:
"Honestly it sounds like you might not be at the stage where this makes sense yet,
and I'd rather be straight with you than waste your time. When you're consistently
at [X milestone], reach back out and we can chat then."

This builds trust and creates a future pipeline. Some of these people WILL come back.

## 2.3 Objection Handling Framework

### OBJECTION #1: "I've been burned by agencies before"
This is the #1 objection for this avatar. Handle it with AGREEMENT, not defense.

Response framework:
"Honestly? I don't blame you. Most agencies in this space are terrible -- they
promise the world, lock you into a 6-month contract, and then deliver garbage
leads that never convert. That's actually WHY we do things differently. We don't
do long contracts. We don't ask you to commit to anything on this call. And the
whole reason I'd want to chat is to show you the actual system so YOU can judge
whether it makes sense. Fair enough?"

Key principles:
- AGREE that agencies are often bad (validates their experience)
- Differentiate by naming the specific things bad agencies do (and you don't)
- Remove the perceived risk (no long contracts, no commitment on the call)
- Put them in control ("so YOU can judge")

### OBJECTION #2: "I don't have the budget right now"
Often this means "I don't trust this will work enough to justify the cost."

Response framework:
"Totally get it -- and that's honestly one of the reasons I wanted to connect.
Most coaches I talk to are in that exact spot where they NEED more revenue to
invest, but they need to invest to GET more revenue. It's a catch-22. On the
call I can walk you through what the numbers actually look like and you can
decide if it makes financial sense. No pressure either way."

If they truly cannot afford it:
"No worries at all. Out of curiosity, what would need to change in your business
over the next 90 days for this to be a no-brainer? [Their answer reveals their
real goal -- note it for future follow-up]"

### OBJECTION #3: "I want to think about it"
This usually means they have an unstated concern.

Response framework:
"Of course, take your time. Just so I can be helpful -- is there something specific
you want to think through? Sometimes I can answer a quick question that saves you
a few days of going back and forth in your head."

This surfaces the REAL objection 70% of the time.

### OBJECTION #4: "Can you just send me more info?"
This is a deflection. They want to avoid a call.

Response framework:
"Totally -- I could send you a doc, but honestly it wouldn't do it justice because
the whole point is that we'd look at YOUR specific situation and I'd show you what
the path looks like for YOUR business. It's not a one-size-fits-all thing. The call
is literally 15-20 minutes and there's zero pitch -- if it's not a fit, I'll tell
you. Would [day] or [day] work better?"

### OBJECTION #5: "I don't want a long contract"
Direct and simple.

Response framework:
"Neither do I. We do [month-to-month / 30-day / performance-based -- whatever your
client's actual terms are]. If we're not delivering, you can walk. I'd rather earn
your business every month than lock you in."

## 2.4 Offer Structure to Reduce Friction

### THE LOW-FRICTION OFFER FRAMEWORK

Given this avatar's skepticism, the offer structure MUST reduce perceived risk
at every level:

**1. No Long-Term Contracts**
- Month-to-month OR 90-day with 30-day exit clause
- If the client insists on 3+ month minimums, frame it as: "We need 90 days to
  build the system properly, but you can cancel with 30 days notice after day 60"

**2. Proof-First Positioning**
- Offer a free strategy session that delivers genuine value (not just a sales pitch)
- Share 2-3 case studies of coaches in the same revenue range who got results
- If possible, offer a "trial" or "pilot" period at reduced scope/cost

**3. Results-Oriented Language**
- Never say: "We'll manage your marketing"
- Instead say: "We'll build you a system that puts 5-10 booked calls on your
  calendar every week"
- Anchor everything to outcomes they care about: booked calls, consistent revenue,
  time back

**4. Risk Reversal**
- Consider a performance guarantee: "If you don't get [X result] in [Y days],
  we'll [refund / work free until you do / etc.]"
- Even a partial guarantee dramatically increases conversion with this audience

**5. Price Anchoring**
- Before revealing price, establish the VALUE: "Most coaches who implement this
  system add $5k-$15k/month within 90 days"
- Then reveal price in context: "The investment is [$X/month], which most clients
  make back from their first 1-2 new coaching clients"
- Break it down: "That's roughly [$X/day] -- less than most coaches charge for
  a single session"

### WHY THE RANGE IS WIDE
Conversion rates vary dramatically based on:
- Quality of targeting (bad targeting = low reply rates regardless of messaging)
- Message personalization (copy-paste = 5% reply; personalized = 20%+ reply)
- Offer strength and pricing (lower price + strong guarantee = higher close rate)
- Setter/closer skill on calls
- Time of year (January/September are peak; June-August are slower)

# SECTION 4: RISK MITIGATION

## 4.1 Instagram Account Restrictions & Bans

### THE RISK
Instagram actively monitors and restricts accounts that exhibit spam-like behavior.
Consequences range from temporary "action blocks" (cannot send DMs for 24-72 hours)
to permanent account suspension.

### PREVENTION PROTOCOL

**Hard Rules (Never Break These):**
1. Never send more than 50-60 total DMs in a single day
2. Never send more than 15-20 DMs in a single hour
3. Never send identical messages to multiple people (even changing one word triggers detection)
4. Never use third-party automation tools that access Instagram's API without authorization
5. Never include external links in the first message (this is a major spam signal)
6. Never send DMs from a brand new account (age the account 2-4 weeks first with organic activity)

**Best Practices:**
1. Warm up the account gradually (see Phase 1 volume targets in Section 1.1)
2. Vary message templates -- have at least 5-7 opening messages and rotate them
3. Mix DM activity with organic activity (post, comment, like, browse) so the account
   looks like a normal user, not a DM bot
4. Space DMs out -- send 3-5, then browse/engage for 10 minutes, then send 3-5 more
5. Use the Instagram mobile app (not desktop or third-party tools) for sending DMs
6. Keep the account in good standing: complete profile, regular posting, real followers

**If You Get Action-Blocked:**
1. STOP all outreach immediately
2. Do not try to "test" if the block is lifted -- wait the full duration
3. For 24-hour blocks: Resume at 50% volume the next day, return to full volume after 3 days
4. For 72-hour blocks: Resume at 25% volume, increase by 25% every 3 days
5. For repeated blocks: The account is flagged. Reduce volume to 15 DMs/day for 2 weeks.
6. Document what you were doing when the block hit (volume, time, message type) to identify triggers

**BACKUP ACCOUNT STRATEGY:**
- Maintain 1-2 backup Instagram accounts that are aged (60+ days) and warmed up
- These accounts should have real content, real followers, and organic activity
- Never use them for outreach unless the primary account is restricted
- This is insurance, not a way to double your volume

## 4.2 Low Response Rates

### IF REPLY RATE IS BELOW 8% (After 200+ DMs Sent)

**Diagnosis checklist:**
1. TARGETING PROBLEM: Are you reaching the right people?
   - Check: What percentage of prospects actually match all 6 qualification criteria?
   - Fix: Tighten qualification. Better to send 15 highly-targeted DMs than 30 mediocre ones.

2. MESSAGE PROBLEM: Is the opening DM compelling?
   - Check: A/B test two completely different message styles for 100 DMs each
   - Fix: Test these angles:
     - Lead with curiosity ("I noticed something about your profile...")
     - Lead with compliment + question ("Your transformation content is incredible -- quick question...")
     - Lead with value ("I put together some ideas for coaches in your space...")
     - Lead with social proof ("Just helped a coach like you go from X to Y...")

3. WARM-UP PROBLEM: Are you skipping the pre-DM engagement?
   - Check: Compare reply rates for warmed-up vs. cold prospects
   - Fix: Implement the 48-hour warm-up rule without exception

4. TIMING PROBLEM: When are you sending?
   - Check: Compare reply rates by time of day and day of week
   - Fix: Concentrate DMs between 10 AM - 1 PM in the prospect's timezone

5. PROFILE PROBLEM: Does the sending account look trustworthy?
   - Check: Would YOU reply to a DM from this profile?
   - Fix: Professional photo, clear bio, recent valuable content, social proof in highlights

### BENCHMARK RESPONSE RATES BY MESSAGE TYPE
- Completely cold (no warm-up, generic message): 3-6%
- Cold with light personalization: 6-10%
- Warm (48-hour engagement + personalized message): 12-20%
- Warm + video DM (Loom or Instagram video message): 18-30%

## 4.3 Prospect Ghosting Mid-Conversation

### WHY THEY GHOST
1. They got busy (most common -- they are solopreneurs running everything)
2. They lost interest/momentum
3. They felt the conversation was turning into a sales pitch
4. They are talking to a competitor simultaneously
5. Instagram buried your message in their requests folder

### THE 3-TOUCH RECOVERY SEQUENCE

**Touch 1 (24 hours after last message):**
Low-pressure, reference the conversation:
"Hey [Name], no worries if you got slammed -- I know how it goes running everything
yourself. Just wanted to circle back on [specific thing you were discussing]. Let me
know when you get a sec."

**Touch 2 (3-4 days after Touch 1):**
Add value, no ask:
"Hey [Name], was thinking about what you mentioned about [their pain point] and
actually came across [relevant tip/resource/idea]. Figured I'd share it either way.
[Brief value point]. Hope things are going well on your end."

**Touch 3 (7 days after Touch 2):**
Permission-based close:
"Hey [Name], I'll keep this short -- I know you're busy. Would it make sense to
circle back on this in a few weeks, or is the timing just not right? Either way,
totally fine. Just don't want to be that person blowing up your inbox."

**After Touch 3 with no reply:** STOP. Move to LOST (reason: ghosted). Set a 60-day
reminder to re-engage with their content and potentially re-open the conversation.

### GHOST PREVENTION
- Keep conversation momentum HIGH. Reply fast.
- Never end a message without a question or clear next step
- Book the call within 3-5 messages. Long DM conversations lose steam.
- Send voice notes or short videos -- they create more personal connection and are
  harder to ignore

## 4.4 Seasonal Patterns in the Fitness Coaching Market

### DEMAND CYCLE

| Period | Demand Level | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| January | PEAK | New Year's resolutions. Coaches are getting flooded with clients and may be too busy to talk about systems. OR they are overwhelmed and desperate for help. Outreach should emphasize: "Now that you have all these new clients, how do you keep them?" |
| February-March | HIGH | Resolution clients are starting to churn. Coaches feel the pain of inconsistency. Great time to outreach with messaging about retention and predictable pipeline. |
| April-May | MODERATE | "Summer body" push. Coaches are moderately busy. Good selling window -- they have revenue but want more consistency. |
| June-July | LOW | Coaches and prospects are on vacation, less engagement. Reduce outreach volume by 20-30%. Focus on relationship building, not hard closes. |
| August | MODERATE-HIGH | "Back to school" energy. Coaches start planning for Q4. Excellent time to sell systems and planning. |
| September-October | HIGH | Fall motivation wave. Second-best selling period. Coaches want to "finish the year strong." |
| November | MODERATE | Pre-holiday slowdown begins. Some coaches start planning for January. Sell future-state: "Be ready for the January rush." |
| December | LOW | Holiday season. Low engagement. Use this time for list building, content creation, and system optimization rather than heavy outreach. |

### TACTICAL ADJUSTMENTS BY SEASON
- January-March: Increase DM volume by 15-20%. This is your highest-conversion window.
- June-July: Decrease DM volume by 20-30%. Focus on nurturing existing pipeline.
- September-October: Increase DM volume by 10-15%. Second wind.
- December: Decrease DM volume by 30-40%. Build prospect lists for January blitz.

Prove the system works. Establish baseline conversion rates. Build the messaging

- Send 20-30 DMs per day (after warm-up period)
- Personally handle every conversation and call
- Obsessively track every metric in the spreadsheet
- A/B test message templates (minimum 100 sends per variant before declaring a winner)
- Document everything: what works, what doesn't, objections heard, questions asked

### TOOLS

- Instagram mobile app
- Calendly (free tier) for booking
- Google Calendar
- Loom (free tier) for video messages

### MONTHLY COST: $0-$50

### EXPECTED RESULTS BY END OF PHASE 1
- 2-5 paying clients acquired

- Documented conversation flow with proven objection responses
- Baseline conversion rates established at every pipeline stage

Move to Phase 2 when ALL of the following are true:
- You have closed at least 3 clients through DM outreach

- You have documented message templates with proven conversion data

### ACTIVITIES

- Create template libraries for every conversation stage (but still personalize each message)
- Build automation for:
  - Prospect list building (partial automation)
  - Follow-up reminders (automated notifications)
  - Call booking and confirmation sequences (automated)
  - Post-call follow-up sequences (automated)
- Develop an SOP document for the entire outreach process (preparation for hiring)
- Start building content that supports inbound leads alongside outbound

### TOOLS (Add to Phase 1 stack)
- GoHighLevel ($97-$297/month) OR HubSpot (free CRM + $20/month for extras)
- Calendly Pro or Cal.com ($12-$20/month)

- Notion ($8-$10/month) for SOPs and knowledge base
- Loom Pro ($12.50/month) for video templates

### MONTHLY COST: $150-$400

### WHAT TO AUTOMATE vs. WHAT TO KEEP MANUAL

| Task | Automate? | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|
| Finding prospect profiles | PARTIALLY -- use tools to surface prospects, manually qualify | Quality control on targeting |
| Pre-DM engagement (likes, comments) | NO -- must be genuine and manual | Instagram detects automated engagement |
| First DM message | NO -- must be personalized | Personalization is the #1 reply rate driver |
| Follow-up sequences (no reply) | PARTIALLY -- automated reminders, manual sends | Can templatize but must still personalize |

| Reporting/metrics | YES -- CRM dashboards | Automated weekly reports |

- Proven message templates with 50+ sends each

- Founder time on outreach reduced to 60-75 minutes/day
- MRR: $7,500-$18,000

### TRANSITION TRIGGER TO PHASE 3
Move to Phase 3 when ALL of the following are true:
- You have a documented SOP that someone else could follow

## Phase 3: Team-Based (Months 6-12)

### OBJECTIVE
Remove the founder from daily outreach execution. Build a team that runs the
system while the founder focuses on sales calls and client delivery.

### HIRING: THE SETTER ROLE

**What a setter does:**
- Executes the daily prospecting and DM outreach
- Follows the proven message templates and conversation flows
- Qualifies prospects using the established criteria
- Books qualified calls on the founder's (or closer's) calendar
- Updates the CRM with all prospect data and notes

**What a setter does NOT do:**
- Run sales calls (that is the closer's job -- initially the founder)
- Create new message templates without approval
- Change the targeting criteria
- Handle objections beyond the documented playbook

**Setter Compensation Models:**
1. Base + Commission: $2,000-$3,000/month base + $50-$150 per qualified call booked that shows up
2. Commission-Only: $200-$500 per qualified call booked that closes (higher per-unit but no base cost)
3. Full-Time Salary: $3,000-$5,000/month (for experienced setters with proven track records)

Recommendation: Start with Model 1 (base + commission). Commission-only attracts
less reliable talent. Full salary is too much risk before the setter proves themselves.

**Where to Find Setters:**
- Beefree, ClosersCafe, or similar sales community job boards
- Twitter/X -- post in sales/marketing communities
- Upwork -- search for "appointment setter" or "Instagram DM outreach"
- Referrals from other agency owners
- AVOID Fiverr for this role (quality is too inconsistent)

**Setter Onboarding (2-Week Ramp):**
- Week 1: Read all SOPs, shadow the founder's outreach, practice messaging with founder feedback
- Week 2: Start sending DMs with founder reviewing every message before it goes out
- Week 3: Send DMs independently, founder reviews end-of-day summary
- Week 4+: Fully independent, weekly performance review

**Setter KPIs:**
- DMs sent per day: 25-30
- Reply rate: >12% (if below, messaging needs work)
- Calls booked per week: 5-8
- Call show rate: >65% (if below, booking/confirmation process needs work)
- Qualified call rate: >70% (if below, qualification process needs work)

### ADDING A CLOSER (Month 9-12)
Once the setter is consistently booking 8+ calls per week, the founder may need
a dedicated closer to handle sales calls.

**Closer Compensation:**
- Base: $3,000-$5,000/month
- Commission: 10-20% of first month's deal value
- Example: $4,000 base + 15% of $1,500 deal = $4,225 per close

**Transition Plan:**
1. Closer shadows the founder on 10-15 calls
2. Founder shadows the closer on 10-15 calls
3. Closer runs calls independently with post-call review
4. Closer is fully independent with weekly performance review

### PHASE 3 COST STRUCTURE
- Setter: $2,500-$4,000/month (base + commissions)
- Tools: $200-$500/month
- Closer (if added): $4,000-$6,000/month (base + commissions)
- Founder time (oversight): 30-45 min/day
- Total: $2,700-$10,500/month

### EXPECTED RESULTS BY END OF PHASE 3
- 15-25 active clients
- Setter booking 6-10 calls per week
- Founder focused on high-value activities (sales calls, strategy, client delivery)
- System runs with minimal daily founder involvement
- MRR: $22,500-$37,500

## APPENDIX A: DM TEMPLATE LIBRARY (Starter Templates)

### OPENING DM TEMPLATES

**Template A: The Observation**
"Hey [Name]! Been following your content for a bit and I love what you're doing
with [specific content type]. Quick question -- are you actively looking to bring
on more coaching clients right now, or is your roster pretty full?"

**Template B: The Mutual Connection**
"Hey [Name], I think we run in similar circles -- I've been working with a few
online coaches helping them systematize their client acquisition. Saw your stuff
on [topic] and it's clear you really know your space. Curious -- what does your
pipeline for new clients look like right now?"

**Template C: The Value Lead**
"Hey [Name]! Random question -- I've been putting together some research on what's
working right now for online fitness coaches to book more discovery calls without
relying on ads. Would something like that be useful for you?"

**Template D: The Social Proof Lead**
"Hey [Name], just helped a coach in a similar space go from sporadic leads to
8-10 booked calls a week. Saw your page and thought this might be relevant for
you too. Is consistent lead flow something you're working on right now?"

**Template E: The Story Reply**
(Use when they post a story about their business, struggle, or wins)
"[Genuine reaction to their story]. That resonated -- I work with online coaches
on exactly this kind of stuff. Are you in a place where you're trying to solve
[problem they alluded to]?"

### FOLLOW-UP TEMPLATES (No Reply)

**Follow-Up 1 (48 hours after initial DM):**
"Hey [Name], know your inbox is probably wild -- just wanted to bump this in case
it got buried. Totally understand if the timing isn't right!"

**Follow-Up 2 (5 days after initial DM):**
"Hey [Name], last thing I'll say and then I'll leave you alone -- I genuinely think
there's something I could help with based on what I see you doing. If you ever
want to chat about [their specific pain point], my door's open."

**Follow-Up 3 (10 days after initial DM):**
"Hey [Name], just circling back one last time. If this isn't on your radar right
now, no worries at all. I'll keep following your stuff regardless -- you're putting
out great content. If anything changes, you know where to find me."

## LEGAL & COMPLIANCE NOTES

**IMPORTANT: This document does not constitute legal advice. Consult with a qualified
attorney for compliance matters.**

Key areas that need professional review:

1. CAN-SPAM / CASL / GDPR: While Instagram DMs are not technically email, privacy
   regulations in different jurisdictions may affect how you can contact people and
   what data you can collect/store. Canada (CASL) is particularly strict. Get legal
   guidance for outreach to Canadian prospects.

2. Instagram Terms of Service: Instagram's TOS prohibit automated messaging and
   spam. All outreach described in this document is manual and personalized.
   However, high-volume messaging may still be considered a violation depending on
   Instagram's interpretation. Review TOS periodically as they change.

3. Testimonials and Claims: Any claims about results (e.g., "$5k-$15k/month
   increase") must be truthful and substantiated. Use disclaimers. Avoid guarantees
   that cannot be backed up. The FTC (US) and equivalent bodies in other countries
   regulate advertising claims, including in DMs.

4. Contract Structure: If offering month-to-month or short-term agreements, have
   a lawyer draft the service agreement. Include clear scope of work, payment terms,
   cancellation terms, and liability limitations.

5. Data Storage: If tracking prospect information in spreadsheets or CRMs, ensure
   you comply with applicable data protection laws (GDPR for UK/EU contacts, CCPA
   for California residents, etc.).
